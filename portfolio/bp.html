The Digital Pod - BP Innovation Centre

Various Activities:  Framework Definition (Design + Agile) +   Workshops  +  User Experience
The Digital Pod is a BP innitiative to apply design framework to all internal projects and products. The main challenge was to increase the company's design maturity using a framework that combined Design Thinking, Lean UX, Agile and DevOps.

The Innovation Centre

01.

Understanding the Problem before Solution

Design Thinking oriented, the innovation centre aimed to offer a new framework for exploring the problem from a different perspective.

02.

Delivering with Lean UX and Agile

Once Design helped to "think outside the box", Lean UX is used to guide the ideas towards development via Agile.

03.

DevOps for Scaling Innovation

Finally, DevOps brings down the barriers between the development and operations parts to achieve and to scale the best customer experience (1).

Design + Agile = Products

During this project design played an important role from user research - interviews, observations and surveys - to user testing and handoff for final delivery using Agile as framework. Techniques applied: User Journey Mapping, User Story Mapping, Hypothesis Canvas, Surveys, User Interviews and Testing & High Fidelity Prototypes.

Enterprise architecture with criativity.

Design for large enterprise is a challenge. Beyond the regular processes, design should consider the complexity of such ecosystem. At the Digital Pod we use different methods and techniques to deal with it.

Remote workshops using Microsoft Surface.

Remote design workshops were carried between two Innovation Centres: London and Huston. Many workshops happened with success. In this example schetches were shared between multiple teams.

Creative Matrix as a method for generating solutions.

For more than 12 months many design methods were applied from Discovery to Delivery. Methods such as Personas, Creative Matrix, User Story Mapping, Abstraction Laddering, Affiny Clustering, among others, became part of a "book of design methods"
